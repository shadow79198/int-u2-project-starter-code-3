A Deadly Risk

It is estimated that around 48.5k people have died in the United States for the use of a gun

In Indiana with data from 2022 it is estimated that around 1.2k people died for the use of a gun

With data from usafacts.org it is estimated that, in 2023, 19 people out of every 100,000 people in Indiana died by a gun-related injury. In comparison states like California it is only 8 out of 100,000 people who died by a gun-related injury.

Why is that?

In order to understand why Indiana has such a high number of people dying due to gun usage, first we need to see how is it that an invididual can access a gun

Lets compare the state of Indiana with the state of California to see the difference on how they regulate gun ownership

In the State of Indiana this are the steps needed to own a gun:

  • go the gun shop
  • pass the background check
  • pay for the gun
  • get an LTCH (license to carry a handgun)(optional)

In the state of California this are the steps needeed to own a gun:

  • Passing the Firearm Safety Certificate
  • Sumbitting an application with a California ID
  • Sumbitting the previous certificate mentioned
  • Paying any fee
  • Wait a mandatory 10-day waiting period
  • Purchase a safe to keep the gun locked
  • Perform a safe handling demostration of the gun

What can be done to have more gun safety in Indiana?

There are some steps that citizens can do to control the gun ownership

Universal background checks

During private purchases of a gun it is not requiered to conduct a background check, this can cause for someone who shouldn't have a gun get access to it. This is why developing a way to have a background check on every invididual, regardless if its a public or private purchase, would increase to lower the number of gun-violence happening in the country

Developing more licenses to own a gun

In Indiana there is no need to show a license to own a gun, which makes it dangerous for everyone, Indiana also doesn't ask for an ID when buying a gun. So the development of licenses and related certificates would improve safety for everyone since those certificates would stop anyone in owning a gun that they would otherwise

Close the Charleston Loophole

The Brady Act, also known as Charleston Loophole because of the Charleston church shooting, allows for gun shops to sell any person a gun if the background check takes longer than 3 business days. This is a highly dangerous practice because most of the time those background checks that takes longer than 3 days are usually rejected, but because of the practice happenig it allows those people to own a gun. The Charleston Loophole has been a controvertial act that some states have regulated agaisnt it. 22 of the 52 states have made some sort of regulation of this act, and only 4 states have fully banned the practice. However this practice should change, because if it doesn't then it would allow many people to own a gun when they shouldn't otherwise
